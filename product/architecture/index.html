Opinionated, because operations work is messy enough already.

Each stage in the appliance is designed to reduce handoffs: collect once, enrich locally, analyze with context, then decide what stays on-prem and what gets forwarded.

Stage 01

Unified collection fabric

Prism Element, Prism Central, Redfish, IPMI, SNMP, HYCU, IPFIX, and NetFlow all enter through one appliance boundary instead of separate collectors and consoles.

  • Prism Element and Prism Central collection
  • Redfish and IPMI hardware collection
  • SNMP polling and trap ingestion
  • HYCU and IPFIX or NetFlow ingestion
Stage 02

Local operations graph

Metrics, logs, dashboards, APIs, and reports live inside one local stack, so teams stop stitching context back together by hand.

  • VictoriaMetrics for metrics
  • VictoriaLogs for logs
  • Grafana for dashboards
  • React and API services behind one HTTPS front door
Stage 03

Cross-domain analysis

Overwatch applies seasonal baselines, anomaly detection, health scoring, and forecasting across infrastructure domains, not just within one data source.

  • 7×24 baseline modeling
  • Anomaly and health score generation
  • Forecasts, reports, and right-sizing signals
  • Cross-layer correlation across backups, network, and compute
Stage 04

Dashboards and forwarding

Operators can work from the local dashboards and reports on the appliance, then forward filtered data to Datadog, Grafana Cloud, or Coralogix if needed.

  • 50+ pre-built dashboards
  • 9 scheduled HTML report types
  • Selective external forwarding
  • Open API for automation and downstream tooling
Stage 05

Operating model

The appliance is designed for on-prem data sovereignty first, with optional managed assistance around it if a team wants help operating the platform.

  • Single local appliance footprint
  • Containerized internal services
  • Local-first storage and retention
  • MaaS available without changing the product architecture
